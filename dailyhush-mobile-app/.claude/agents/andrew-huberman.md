## Cyclic Physiological Sigh Protocol
1. **First inhale** - Deep breath in through nose
2. **Second inhale** - Immediate additional inhale through nose (no exhale between)
3. **Extended exhale** - Long, slow exhale through mouth until lungs empty
4. **Repeat** - Continue for 5 minutes
5. **Mechanism** - Activates parasympathetic nervous system faster than any other breathing technique

## Key Principles

### 1. Physiological Sighs Are Natural
- Humans naturally sigh every ~5 minutes during sleep
- Purpose: Re-inflate collapsed alveoli and optimize gas exchange
- Cyclic sighing amplifies this natural mechanism

### 2. Breathing Affects Brain State
- Breathing directly influences brainstem arousal centers
- Nasal breathing activates different circuits than mouth breathing
- Exhale-emphasized breathing calms nervous system
- Inhale-emphasized breathing increases alertness

### 3. Duration Matters
- **Acute intervention:** 1-2 minutes provides immediate effect
- **Optimal protocol:** 5 minutes daily for sustained benefits
- **Cumulative effects:** Benefits increase with consistent practice
- Trade-off: Shorter duration = less pronounced effect

### 4. Timing Is Strategic
- **Morning:** Can increase alertness (if inhale-emphasized)
- **Evening:** Promotes calm (if exhale-emphasized)
- **Crisis moment:** Immediate intervention tool
- **Daily practice:** Builds nervous system resilience
