## üéØ ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS FOR DAILYHUSH

### 1. REFRAME THE QUESTION
**Wrong question:** "How do we make techniques faster?"
**Right question:** "How do we match technique duration to intervention type?"

### 2. SEGMENTATION BY GOAL

**Rapid Physiological Calm (Breathing)**
- Use: Acute panic, physiological arousal
- Duration: 5 minutes (Huberman protocol)
- Techniques: Cyclic Sigh, Box Breathing
- No typing: Pure physiological intervention

**Cognitive Interruption (Rumination)**
- Use: Overthinking, worry loops, rumination
- Duration: 90-120 seconds (Watkins research)
- Techniques: Cognitive Reframe, Grounding
- Typing required: Cognitive engagement is the mechanism

**Awareness Building (Body/Emotion)**
- Use: Physical tension, emotional processing
- Duration: 5-20 minutes (Kabat-Zinn protocols)
- Techniques: Full Body Scan, Emotional Awareness
- Guided attention: Sustained practice

### 3. HONEST POSITIONING

**Don't say:** "Interrupt your spiral in 60 seconds"
**Do say:** "Begin interrupting your spiral in 60 seconds" (with expectation of full protocol)

**Don't say:** "Quick fix for anxiety"
**Do say:** "Evidence-based intervention matched to your need"

### 4. ADAPTIVE ALGORITHM UPDATES

Match technique to situation:
- **Acute panic** ‚Üí Cyclic Sigh (5 min version)
- **Rumination** ‚Üí Cognitive Reframe (120s) or Grounding (90s)
- **Physical tension** ‚Üí Body Scan (20 min) or Rapid Body Check (5 min)
- **First-time user** ‚Üí Shorter intro versions
- **Engaged user** ‚Üí Full protocols

### 5. PERCEIVED VALUE DESIGN

High perceived value requires:
1. **Substantial duration** (not too quick)
2. **Active engagement** (typing, thinking)
3. **Expert validation** (show research credentials)
4. **Progress indicators** (show depth of work)
5. **Completion satisfaction** (acknowledge effort)
