Day 3: The Pattern

You're not overthinking. You're replaying.

Quick question:

When you took the quiz and saw those three phrases—"I should've known better," "Afraid of doing it wrong," "Can't stop replaying"—did you think: "Wait, that's exactly what I say to myself"?

That's the pattern I recognized when I analyzed 296 quiz responses.

Here's what those three phrases actually mean:

"I should've known better" = self-punishment disguised as analysis

"Afraid of doing it wrong" = fear of being exposed as inadequate

"Can't stop replaying" = compulsive loop your nervous system now expects

This isn't about thinking differently. It's about interrupting a nervous system pattern.

I couldn't stop replaying how I came across in conversations. Did they see I was faking it? Did that email expose that I don't know what I'm doing? Did I sound stupid in that meeting?

For 8 years, I tried:

  • Meditation (made me more aware of the thoughts)
  • Journaling (I just spiraled on paper)
  • Therapy (helpful, but $150/session adds up)
  • CBT techniques (too slow when I'm already activated)

Nothing worked because they all targeted the thoughts. But by the time you're aware you're spiraling, your nervous system already decided you're unsafe 7 seconds ago.

That's why I created F.I.R.E.

It targets the 7-second window before the replay loop locks in. When 296 women wrote those same three phrases I'd been saying—I knew this protocol needed to exist.
