üçÇ The 4-Week Gradual Transition Method
The Golden Rule: Never change more than one product at a time. Your skin needs to adapt gradually, not shock into submission.
Week 1: Assess and Prepare

The Skin Audit I Do Every Season

Take photos in natural light (same time, same angle) and honestly assess:
  • Texture: Rough patches appearing? (Sign you need gentle exfoliation)
  • Tone: Looking dull or uneven? (Time for vitamin C adjustment)
  • Hydration: Tight after cleansing? (Moisture barrier needs support)
  • Sensitivity: Stinging with usual products? (Reduce actives temporarily)
My Results This Year: Summer had left me with clogged pores but also some dehydration‚Äîa tricky combo that needed careful handling.
Week 2: The First Swap

Cleanser Transition: Gel to Cream

Summer Hero: Cetaphil Daily Facial Cleanser (gel formula)
Fall Transition: Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser (cream formula)
Cost: Same price, different formula

I gradually mixed both cleansers for a week before fully switching. This prevented the "tight skin" feeling that usually comes with seasonal cleanser changes.

Pro Tip: If you love your summer cleanser, just reduce usage from twice daily to once, adding a gentler morning cleanse.
Week 3: Moisture Boost

The Hydration Sandwich Technique

Instead of switching moisturizers completely, I started layering:

Layer 1: Hyaluronic acid serum on damp skin
Layer 2: My summer moisturizer (Neutrogena Hydro Boost)
Layer 3: 3 drops of face oil (The INKEY List Squalane Oil)

Total Additional Cost: $9 for the oil
Results: 40% improvement in skin hydration (measured with a skin scanner) without changing my core routine
