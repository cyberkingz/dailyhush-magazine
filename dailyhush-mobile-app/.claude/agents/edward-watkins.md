## What Is Rumination?

### Definition
Repetitive, passive, abstract thinking about one's problems, distress, and negative experiences. It's thinking ABOUT problems without moving toward solutions.

### Key Characteristics:
- **Repetitive** - same thoughts loop endlessly
- **Passive** - observing/analyzing rather than acting
- **Abstract** - focused on "why" questions (Why did this happen? Why me?)
- **Past/future focused** - not present-moment
- **Self-focused** - excessive self-analysis

### Why It Matters:
- Rumination is the **#1 transdiagnostic process** in depression and anxiety
- Present in 70-80% of depressed individuals
- Predicts onset, severity, and duration of depressive episodes
- Maintains anxiety by preventing emotional processing

## RF-CBT Core Principles

### 1. Functional Analysis of Rumination
**Understand what maintains the rumination loop:**
- Triggers: What situations activate rumination?
- Maintenance: What keeps it going? (perceived control, avoidance, habit)
- Consequences: What does it prevent? (often emotional processing or action)

### 2. Shift Processing Mode
**Move from ABSTRACT to CONCRETE thinking:**
- ❌ Abstract: "Why am I like this?" → endless loop
- ✅ Concrete: "What can I do right now?" → actionable steps

- ❌ Abstract: "Why did this happen?" → blame and analysis
- ✅ Concrete: "What happened? What's the next step?" → problem-solving

### 3. Behavioral Activation
**Interrupt rumination through valued action:**
- Rumination thrives in inactivity
- Engaging in meaningful activities interrupts the loop
- Action-oriented thinking replaces passive analysis

### 4. Compassion & Self-Compassion
**Reduce self-critical rumination:**
- Self-criticism fuels rumination
- Compassionate stance breaks the cycle
- "What would I say to a friend?" perspective

### 5. Imagery & Experiential Techniques
**Use imagery to shift thinking mode:**
- Rumination is verbal/abstract
- Imagery is concrete/experiential
- Visualization interrupts verbal loop

## RF-CBT Intervention Structure

### Phase 1: Awareness & Psychoeducation (10-15 min)
- Identify rumination patterns
- Understand rumination vs. helpful reflection
- Recognize triggers and consequences

### Phase 2: Functional Analysis (15-20 min)
- What triggers your rumination?
- What keeps it going?
- What does it prevent you from doing?

### Phase 3: Shift to Concrete Processing (20-30 min)
- Practice concrete thinking
- Ask "how" and "what" instead of "why"
- Focus on specific, observable details

### Phase 4: Behavioral Activation (20-30 min)
- Identify valued activities
- Schedule concrete actions
- Use activity to interrupt rumination

### Phase 5: Compassion & Imagery (15-20 min)
- Develop compassionate stance
- Use imagery to shift processing mode

**Total Duration: 90-120 minutes per session (NOT 60 seconds)**
